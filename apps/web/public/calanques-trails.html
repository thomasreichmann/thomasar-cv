Spot 1 · easy staircase

l'Arène & Corton

Two wild pebble coves at the foot of the red Cap Canaille cliff, side by side, sharing one parking lot. Reached from the town side (avenue du Revestel) — so they stay open even on a red fire-risk day. Park once, look at both, pick whichever is quieter.

Where to park

Aim straight for Parking de la Madie on avenue du Revestel, on the eastern edge of Cassis (~150 paid spaces, 4-hour max). It's the realistic lot and sits right above both coves. Midweek in late June you've got a fair shot at noon — and the 4-hour cap means cars keep cycling out.

Ignore the tiny cul-de-sac lot right at l'Arène cove (~10–20 cars, last 50 m closed to traffic) — it's full from mid-morning regardless of season, because it's that small.

Spot 2 · coastal walk

Calanque de Port-Pin

The postcard one: a turquoise inlet where Aleppo pines grow down to a small pebble beach — shady, sheltered, genuinely beautiful, and a swim-friendly gentle entry. It's a real 25–35 min walk each way from the trailhead at Port-Miou, and it sits inside the national park's fire-risk zone, which is the catch (see the warning below).

Check this before you drive — it's make-or-break

Port-Pin is in the regulated massif. From 1 June, the park sets a daily fire level: on an orange day the trails are open only 6–11am, and on red/black the massif is closed all day. A noon arrival on an orange day means you'll be turned back at the trailhead. Open the Mes Calanques fire map first — only go if today is green. Otherwise take the coves above (town-side, always open).

Where to park

Closest lot: Parking de la Presqu'île at the end of avenue Notre-Dame / des Calanques in Cassis (~500 spaces, ~€8–10/day, cash) — it's the only one a road reaches, but it shows “complet” early and the single access road can crawl. At noon, assume it's full or queueing.

The reliable move: use Relais des Gorguettes (free, at the A50 Cassis exit) and ride the navette shuttle (~€1.60 round trip, every 15–20 min) toward La Presqu'île / Port-Miou. No parking gamble — just adds a transfer.

Presqu'île lot ↗ Gorguettes (reliable)

The trail: Port-Miou → Port-Pin

An easy, well-marked coastal path — roughly 2–2.3 km / 25–35 min one way, with a couple of short rocky, uneven sections and a slippery pebble descent at the end. Wear trainers, not flip-flops. Carry water — there's nothing out there.

1
Start at the Port-Miou gateFrom the Presqu'île parking (or the shuttle stop), walk to the barrier at the end of avenue des Calanques — the signed pedestrian entrance into the park.
2
Walk the length of Port-MiouFollow the wide, flat gravel track along the fjord-like inlet, past the moored boats, to the far end.⏱ ~10–12 min · flat
3
Climb over to Port-PinAt the end of Port-Miou, a marked path (blue/GR markers) rises over a low rocky saddle through the pines and drops toward the next inlet.⏱ ~12–18 min · some rock
4
Descend to the beachA short, slippery pebble/rock descent brings you to the small Port-Pin cove. Gentle, calm entry — among the best swimming of the calanques.

◆ If it's closed or full

Fire-closed / orange day: don't even drive — go to l'Arène & Corton instead (town-side, always open). Jump up ↑

Open but can't park & don't want the shuttle: Port-Miou itself is a flat ~10 min walk from the same trailhead — less of a swim, but you're in the park in minutes. Or fall back to Grande Plage, La Ciotat for guaranteed parking + sand. Map ↗

Quick logic: green fire day & you want the prettiest swim → Port-Pin via the Gorguettes shuttle. Any other day, or you want it simple → the Cap Canaille coves from La Madie, with Grande Plage as the safety net.
