The fast way to Ferney runs inland — Aix, then up through Grenoble, Chambéry and Annecy to Geneva (~4h45 with no stops). That means the big alpine lakes sit right near the end of your drive. Here's the best swim stop for the smallest detour — plus the spectacular options if you want to make a day of it.

Lac d'Aiguebelette

Savoie, near Chambéry · late in drive
WowWarm swimMotorboat-free

An emerald, silent, swimmably-warm lake the international crowds miss.

Why it winsOne of the warmest, cleanest natural lakes in France (~20°C already in June). Motorboats banned since 1967, so it's genuinely silent — vivid emerald water under limestone cliffs, two little islands you can paddle to. Far quieter than Annecy or Le Bourget.
Detour & parkingJust west of Chambéry — ~19 min off the A41, ~38 min round-trip. Lakeside parking is free; arriving late afternoon you've missed the morning rush. Aim for Plage du Sougey (prettiest, lawns, restaurant).
Good to knowLifeguarded beaches with showers & SUP hire; token entry fee (~€4), free after 19:00. Swimming only at the 7 marked beaches. ~1h20 left to Ferney afterward.

If you want jaw-dropping

The Verdon option

The most spectacular water of all — turquoise lakes at the mouth of Europe's grandest canyon. The cost: it's early in the drive and adds ~1–1.5h, making it a long day. Worth it if the journey is the point.

+60min · early

Lac d'Esparron

Esparron-de-Verdon · the quiet one
Pearl of VerdonWarmest ~26°CLess mobbed

The intimate, wilder cousin of Sainte-Croix — cliffs, coves, electric boats only.

Why goTurquoise reservoir hemmed by limestone cliffs, the warmest water in the Verdon, a supervised village beach plus quiet coves a short walk away. Rent a little electric boat into the lower gorges. Far calmer than the famous spots.
Detour & parking~30 min off the A51 near Manosque, ~60 min round-trip, early in your drive (~12:30). Village lots (one free, others paid weekends); arrive-by-noon is wise on a Saturday.

Lac de Sainte-Croix

Les Salles-sur-Verdon · the famous turquoise
IconicPaddle the gorge

The postcard turquoise lake at the mouth of the Gorges du Verdon.

Why goVivid turquoise, electric-boat-only, with the famous Pont du Galetas where you paddle a kayak straight into the canyon walls. Use the quieter beaches at Les Salles-sur-Verdon or Bauduen, not the mobbed Galetas bridge.
Detour & parking~37 min off-route, ~70 min round-trip, early in the drive. Best parking odds at Les Salles-sur-Verdon (shaded, supervised). The Galetas car parks are a gamble and packed.
Directions

Reality: the Verdon falls ~1h15 into the drive, so you'd swim ~12:30 then still have ~4h to Ferney — a big but spectacular day.

Lake Annecy

Talloires / Angon · ~30 min from Ferney corridor
FamousCleanest in Europe

The iconic one — turquoise water against alpine peaks, if you'll brave the crowds.

Why goBilled as the cleanest lake in Europe; top-tier turquoise-against-mountains scenery and a gorgeous old town. Swim at Angon (calmer/leafy) rather than the saturated Talloires main beach.
The catchIt's the most crowded option, and a Saturday makes the lakeside D1508 and parking genuinely painful. Aiguebelette is the quieter, easier version of the same idea — that's why it's the pick.

Lac de Paladru

Charavines, Isère · just off the A48
Quick dipLowest detour

The lowest-effort dip — turquoise "blue lake" barely off the motorway.

Why goNaturally turquoise (chalky lakebed), warms to ~25°C, supervised beaches — and only ~12 min off the A48 near Grenoble, mid-drive. A fast, easy leg-stretch swim.
The catchPleasant rather than spectacular, and busy on a Saturday. One real caveat: it occasionally gets cyanobacteria swim bans — check the beach status before counting on a swim.
