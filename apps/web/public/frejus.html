The Estérel coast hides its best swimming in small red-porphyry calanques — wilder and clearer than the wide town sands. The catch is the same as always: tiny lots that fill by late morning. So this is ranked parking-first — one guaranteed sure-thing up top, then the scenic coves worth a small gamble, plus where to go clothing-optional.

Go mid-morning if you can Weekday + late June = shoulder All within ~30 min of Fréjus

Good news on fire closures

Late June, the Estérel & Maures massifs fall under daily fire-risk rules that can shut forest trails — but every beach below is reached directly from the coast road, not a forest path, so they stay open even on a red day. Only matters if you'd planned an inland Estérel hike; for the swims, you're clear. Quick check: Var fire-risk map.

The good stuff · 2–4

Red-rock coves

The reason to be on this coast: little porphyry calanques with turquoise, snorkel-clear water. Smaller lots — arrive mid-morning, or have the sure thing as backup.

A pocket cove funnelled by deep-red cliffs into a calm, clear lagoon.

The parking truthThe Tiki Plage lot sits right above the cove but is small and saturates from ~11:00 in peak summer. Late June is better — but arrive by ~10:30 to be safe, or fall back to Base Nature.
Swim & sceneThe rock funnel kills the swell, so it's calm, shallow and clear — a lovely couple's swim. Golden sand, gravelly in patches; Île d'Or out front.
Good to knowSeasonal showers/toilets, a well-liked beach restaurant on the sand, lifeguard in season. Sea-edge access — open on red fire days.

Wild & quiet · 5–6

If you want seclusion

Furthest out and the trickiest parking, but the quietest, wildest water — plus one sandy Gulf beach that has a parking backup built in.

The parking truthRoughly 10 roadside spots in the bend — that's it. The tiny capacity is exactly what keeps it empty. Only realistic before ~9:30, or get lucky on turnover. This one is a true gamble.
Swim & sceneCrystal-clear, sheltered turquoise water and outstanding snorkelling around a wild headland with pines, a Roman fishpond and a WWII bunker. Rock/pebble entry — water shoes.
Good to knowWild and unsupervised — no services on the point; shaded picnic area in the middle. Bring water and lunch.

You asked · clothing-optional

Naturist-friendly

The Var is France's #2 naturist department. Your nearest options, from easy-sandy to wild-red-rock.

★Easiest

Esclamandes

Saint-Aygulf, Fréjus · ~12 min
Naturist zoneSandy

A sandy bay with a marked naturist section, backed by a nature reserve.

🅿️ Park
Gamble · paid
🥾 Walk
~3 min flat
🚗 Drive
~12 min
The parking truthThe Esclamandes lot is paid in summer and often full by 10:00. Backups: the two RN98 lots (Saint-Aygulf I & II, ~420 spaces) or the village + summer shuttle. Weekday earlier-is-better.
Swim & sceneLong sandy bay, gentle gradual entry, lifeguarded — easy family-calm water. The beach is zoned (family / naturist), so the clothing-optional stretch is signed.
Good to knowToilets, snack kiosks, lifeguards mid-June to Sept. Coastal nature reserve behind. Open regardless of fire risk.
Directions

◆ Two more, for red-rock seclusion

Le Dramont (Rochers du Dramont, ~15 min) — naturist zones on the rocks behind the “Cathedral rocks” and near Pointe du Dramont, with the Île d'Or view. Le Trayas (~25–30 min) — the most officially naturist Estérel cove, in a small red-rock inlet between Pointe du Maubois and Cap Roux. Both are scenic but rocky-entry with small, early-filling parking — go early and bring water shoes.
